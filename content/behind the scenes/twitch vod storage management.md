on february 20th, 2025, twitch announced that they would be implementing [a 100 hour storage limit for highlights and uploads](https://help.twitch.tv/s/article/video-on-demand?language=en_US#storage).

> [!danger] on storage:
> this decision from twitch is a great reminder of why it's important to:
> 1. **take responsibility over your data and files.** do not rely on a corporation to be the sole host of your files, as you cannot guarantee that they have the same interests and values as you. neglecting to back up your files incurs the risk of losing it forever.
> 2. **never assume that things will continue existing as they are today for all eternity.** my VODs have been on twitch for a very long time because i naively assumed that this option would always be available to me.

remember,  **convenience does not equal freedom.** while it can be a pain in the ass to go out of your way to do things that are a little more time consuming/requires more resources, it is very empowering to take responsibility for the choices you make. in the case of backing up your files and VODs specifically, you get the security of knowing your files are safe, and the freedom to move them wherever you like. and that's a wonderful thing!

## actually wait, i changed my mind
after i finished my friend's website and my class, my opinion on saving the VODs changed. these two experiences were very transformative and i'm super grateful to have been a part of both of those!!! but also, they also took up most of my waking hours and resources.

with significantly less things to do on my plate, i had a moment of clarity and realized, "hey, even if i am past my streaming era, i still want to try and save the artifacts of my past self while i have the chance."

am i embarking on a fool's errand because of the sheer amount of footage? perhaps. but i don't think it's foolish to want to save and protect the things that are important to you.

when it comes to having things, i usually prefer the approach of "less is more." the exception to this, to me, are things that are irreplaceable, and imbued with your own unique touch. twitch VODs are exactly that, which is why i'm keen on preserving them while i can.

## the person that i used to be
i remember streaming once a week on thursdays (or tuesday) from december 2020 to july 2022.

i originally started streaming because i needed something to help me start and finish persona 5 royal (P5R). at this point of my life, i was very much a workaholic; i'd always be darting between schoolwork and fanart with no time for games. because of this, the only way i could see myself finishing P5R was to... turn it into something my brain perceived as "productive work," AKA streaming. (and don't worry!!! these days i understand the dangers of this mentality and i'm trying my best to unlearn it...)

the average P5R stream would last for ten hours. i think this metric alone speaks volumes about how obsessed i was with persona 5, lol.

i really loved the characters of persona 5, and my twitch chat did too. over time, i ended up fostering a community of people who liked pingponging headcanons at each other non-stop. at the time, i really loved being a part of these type of discussions and it really furthered my appreciation of the characters.

i also remember eating mcdonalds happy meals on nearly every stream because it was fast to eat and gave me energy to continue streaming. typing this sentence out makes me realize, "wow, i really only cared about efficiency, huh." (i have not had mcdonald's since... late 2023 [because of the boycott](https://bdsmovement.net/whattoboycott), but also, i really don't need to be putting that kind of thing in my body.)

P5R (and by extension, the streams), consumed me whole, and was my entire world. i distinctly remember thinking, "wow, i never want this feeling of excitement to go away. i love the community i have and how creative everyone is. i'm so happy that we can all bond over goro akechi and akira kurusu." looking back, i feel that this desire for P5R to last for all eternity can be likened to experiencing love for the first time...

while P5R was what kickstarted my weekly stream routine, i kept playing games on the internet because i loved talking with my community. i did playthroughs of mario odyssey, persona 5 strikers, IB, pokemon shining pearl, persona 3 dancing, sonic colors, xenoblade x, and super mario galaxy 2, up until july 2022. all of which, had some discussion of persona in the chat, even though it had literally nothing to do with what i was playing.😂 but it made me very happy at that time...

as i reminisce on these experiences, it is very clear to me that **i will probably never be that person ever again.** even though the weekly streams were very near and dear to my heart, the mere thought of doing that nowadays exhausts me. i'm glad that these days, i know myself well enough to approach streaming in a more fulfilling and sustainable way. nevertheless, i cannot deny that my experiences with my community were very formative in how i carry myself today... i wouldn't be the same person without them! 🥺

it is because i want to honor my past selves and the people i met along the way that i am taking on this endeavor to preserve the VODs that i can. i've only been working on this for a few days, but it's given me so much appreciation and compassion for what i've done so far. i'm really happy to be doing this...! (it's also prompted me to start backing up and reorganizing the rest of the files on my computer as well...)

## how i'm downloading and organizing my VODs
now that i've gotten most of my sentimental/mushy feelings out there... i can actually start talking about the actual process. YIPPEE!!!

i will probably continue using this because it's the easiest for me to understand...

### keeping track of what i need to download
because this is an endeavor that will take several days, i decided to take the time to make a spreadsheet for my VODs. (I LOVE SPREADSHEETS!!! MY BELOVED!!!)

usually i'd use Google Sheets for this, but this time i decided to use Microsoft excel for a few reasons:
- twitch enforcing a storage limit makes me more keen on saving my files locally
- i'm a little sick of Google's push for AI (understatement) and the amount of data they collect (also an understatement).
- microsoft excel at least lets you [turn off AI training](https://medium.com/illumination/ms-word-is-using-you-to-train-ai-86d6a4d87021)

 as a side note, if you don't want to use the Google and Microsoft suites, [LibreOffice](https://www.libreoffice.org/) is an open source alternative! i've heard from a friend that the excel equivalent is a little cumbersome to work with, which is unfortunate, but maybe someday i'll learn the ins and outs of the suite.

i have four sheets on the `.xlsx` file:
- **VOD LIST:** this catalogs all of the VODs on my twitch channel. it includes information like:
	- the date of the original stream,
	- the title of the stream
	- the category
	- the duration of the stream
	- and an indicator of whether or not it was saved on my computer
- **PRIORITY:** this is a sheet that i made to help get my priorities straight. it lists the categories i streamed, and how much time each VOD cumulatively takes up. it helped me ask questions like:
	- which series do i want to keep on twitch (within the 100 hour limit)?
	- which series do i care the most about downloading?
- **STORAGE:** this sheet is used to track how much GB each category/series takes on my hard drive, relative to the length of the total VODs + the resolutions they're streamed at. this data helps me properly assess how much storage i need by allowing me to make predictions on how much space a given series will take.
- **PROGRESS LOG:** a sheet logging down which VODs i downloaded (using the same metrics from VOD LIST) and when. it's great for keeping track of my pace and seeing the total hours downloaded.

### maintaining pace
at the end of a download session, i run some calculations to figure out how many videos i need to download each day to make sure i meet the deadline... the formula is something like:

> [!info] the formula:
> (174 - x - y) / z
>
> in which:
> - 174 is the number of videos i started with
> - x is the number videos downloaded so far (based on **PROGRESS LOG** sheet)
> - y is the number of videos that i plan on keeping on twitch. currently it's 35, but it may change over time...
> - z is the number of days until April 17th, my self-imposed deadline (to account for any timezone differences. april 17th is included in this number.)

and in practice, using today's download session as an example...

>[!example] march 26th downloads:
>- (174 - 34 - 35)/22
>- 105/22
>- 4.7~ videos a day

i'm happy with this number, since it was at 7 at the start of yesterday...!!! since i've started, i've been downloading well over the recommended amount, which is nice because:
- it's entirely possible i'll finish earlier than expected, which i'd be happy with!
- it gives me buffers on days i am not able to download VODs for whatever reason

(and yes, i'm fully aware that my enthusiasm for this can fade over time. but thats OK because i've been prioritizing the most important VODs first rather than doing this aimlessly!)

honestly, downloading has been a very chill and cozy process! i kind of wish i started this earlier, but i understand why i didn't... i was busy, but also, it's hard to stay motivated without a proper structure.

and that's exactly why the spreadsheet is so important: **it acts as a measurable goalpost that doesn't move.** measurable goals are the key to success, and for me, spreadsheets are the perfect way of helping me visualize and document my progress. they're easy to sort and comprehend, what's not to love?

## the future of streaming on twitch (for me)
given the nature of this endeavor... it really prompted me to think about whether or not i *want* to continue streaming on twitch, and if i do, how i want to go about it.

after i said goodbye to the weekly schedule of streams in july 2022, i would only stream whenever i felt like it. and i will continue to approach streams in this manner. given my shift in priorities, i think it's very unlikely for me to stream. it's not impossible for me to do a stream (as i do tend to cycle around to old hobbies every now and again), but i can't really see them being a priority like it was in 2020-2022.

as i get older, i find that i really enjoy having time away from the internet, especially the public-facing parts of it. if the pandemic taught me anything, it's that being plugged into the internet 24/7 is a great way to make yourself feel mentally exhausted. even if you curate your feeds of information to be aligned with your values, it's still really important to have something that grounds you in three-dimensional space, y'know?

being able to draw a clear boundary between the public and private aspects of your life is a really important thing to practice, especially on the internet. i find this difficult sometimes, because the internet brings out my more extroverted tendencies, but that makes time offline all the more important. i hope that everyone remembers that you can still show up to conversations on the internet and be kind to others, while protecting your own privacy (and mental sanity). i strongly encourage you to find a balance that works for you.

## what i'd like to do with the VODs...
if it's not readily apparent, downloading these VODs has been a personal endeavor first and foremost. i'm not really interested in preserving them for the public's consumption and reuploading them elsewhere. and i feel that any future streams i do will be like that too; you either saw it or you didn't.

i don't know why, but there's something appealing to me about the VODs fading sands of time. maybe being on the internet for so much of my life, where there's receipts and documents of every conversation ever, has made me seek out pockets of time that are fleeting, and transient. to put it in other words, my thought process is, "well, if this isn't going to be around forever, i want to try my best to appreciate the moment while it lasts."

as i've been downloading my VODs, i realized something very ironic: making these full-length playthroughs available to visit at "any point in the future" made it harder for me to commit the experiences to memory. this could have been caused from streaming so much, without ever stopping to look back and properly appreciate what i did. or maybe it's because streaming demands that you multitask, and it's very easy for your attention span to be diluted. or both, who knows!

that said, i have found out in the past year or two that:
1. i enjoy editing down long hours of footage in highlight reels, and...
2. i generally find those easier to revisit than a whole playthrough (which i will only really watch once and then never again)

something about video editing in this manner scratches an itch in my brain the same way journaling does... i think it's because both hobbies force you to be selective about what you feel is most important to remember and look back on. limitations can breed creativity!

>[!warning] disclaimer:
> while video editing can allow you to make cool artifacts and memoirs, it's important to do it selectively. if you aren't selective about what you edit, and spend all of your time editing videos of past experiences, it takes away opportunities to live in the present. **you cannot do everything and should not strive to do that,** and this is especially pronounced with video editing because it's insanely easy to accumulate a backlog of long hours of footage.

i write this disclaimer because of my personal experiences. in my experience, trying to save the details of anything and everything at *all times* will do more harm than good. if everything is important, nothing is important. while it sounds nice on paper to give your 100% in every pursuit of your life, this can very easily lead to burnout. i wish i could tell my past self, "**you can still put your best foot forward without having to go ham on everything. please give your mind and body time to replenish it's well of energy.**."

this entire document is very rambly, but...!!! downloading twitch VODs really got me existential, haha. it's prompted me to reevaluate so many of my habits, both in regards to keeping my files backed up, and living in the present moment. maybe i don't need to constantly be striving for more, or moving on from one project to the next. it's OK to take moments of pause from my crafts...!!!

maybe in the future, after i get all the VODs downloaded, i'll edit down some of the series i really enjoyed streaming into a highlight reel... or not...! and that's OK with me, because i'm just happy to have the footage in some capacity (even if it makes my storage cry LOL). i don't need to make anything grand out of my VOD footage for it to be worth saving.
